Hiking

Friendly Vistas Trail

This trail meanders along the shoreline of Jarvis Lake. It goes through mixed wood forests and the scenic Jarvis Creek wetlands. Various viewpoints with rest areas are located along the trail, creating excellent birding and wildlife viewing opportunities. Friendly Vista Trail is good for wheelchairs and strollers. It is accessed from the southwest end of Kelley’s Bathtub Trail.

Alberta Forest Services Museum

The Hinton Training Centre (HTC) provides natural resource training for clients in government and industry. Operated by the Alberta government, the Centre is unique among educational institutions in the province. Since 1960, it has developed and delivered professional technical training programs in wildfire management and forest management. The HTC campus is located on a 14-hectare area in the middle of Hinton, a town in the central Foothills region of Alberta. It includes an Academic Building, 2 residences, a fitness room, rappel tower, wildfire lookout tower, vehicle service garage, and self-guided museum and walking trail. Facilities can be booked by the Alberta government and other organizations. Find out more about HTC programs and services.

Wildlife Viewing

Beaver Boardwalk

The Beaver Boardwalk, world’s longest freshwater boardwalk is a great family outing that showcases the local wetland system around Maxwell Lake. Built over several years through volunteer effort with corporate support, the attraction features more than three kilometres of boardwalk, seating areas, and interpretive signs. Numbers of beavers vary from year to year, but at times there are upwards of a dozen in the area. The boardwalk meanders through marshland and fens, bringing visitors close to the active beaver dam and lodge.
