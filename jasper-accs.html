Hotels

Fairmont Jasper Park Lodge

Fairmont Jasper Park Lodge, situated in Alberta's magnificent Jasper National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, invites you to experience their ultimate cabin escape, surrounded by nature and abundant wildlife. Spread out over 700 acres, this year-round luxury mountain resort wraps around the shores of pristine Lac Beauvert and Canada's #1 Golf Resort. Choose from private Signature Cabins (featuring your own kitchen, living space, patio, and more) or one of our many quaint cabin-style rooms throughout the expansive property. Fairmont Jasper Park Lodge has a deep commitment to sustainability and was awarded the Hotel Association of Canada's prestigious Green Key Environmental Award for 2017.

Becker's Chalets Jasper

Becker's Chalets was established in the 1940s. After more than 50 years of development, Becker's Chalets has grown from 42 chalets to the first-class family operation of today with 118 chalets, dedicated to the comfort and cleanliness of your stay within the Rocky Mountains. With 23 different room types to offer; ranging from 1 and 2-bedroom chalets to the larger log chalets that accommodate up to 10 people. Campgrounds

Wabasso Campground

With pristine views, rushing river water and electrical sites for small RVs, Wabasso Campground in Jasper National Park lets you get away from it all. This quiet campground is tucked away along 93A, a secondary highway offering excellent road biking opportunities. This is a good starting area for overnight treks into the backcountry and shorter day trips to Moab Lake and Athabasca Falls. Wabasso is best suited for tenting and for motorhomes and trailers less than 27 feet. It features heated bathrooms with lights, flush toilets, and hot and cold running water.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$32 CAD

Whistler's Campground

Whistlers Campground in Jasper National Park's largest campground with 781 sites and modern amenities including hot and cold running water, showers and electrical and full hook-up sites for RVs. If you are looking for a unique camping experience, try their oTENTiks. Bring your own food, drinks, axe and bedding. Parks Canada supplies the structure, equipped with electricity, heaters, bunk beds and sleeping pads. A new registration centre is open complete with three all-gender accessible washrooms and six service desks. Eighteen new combined washroom and shower facilities have been added throughout the campground and campsites have been improved with new picnic tables, fire pits, and great views. Visitors are encouraged to BYOB (Bring Your Own Bike or rent one) and leave their vehicles behind. From Whistlers and Wapiti Campgrounds, a family-friendly connector trail is just a short 20-minute bike ride to the townsite and also connects to popular day-use areas such as Lake Edith and Lake Annette, Maligne Canyon. The Bear’s Paw Espresso food truck will be serving up delicious treats and coffee to campers throughout the day.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$24 CAD

Wapiti Campground

On the shore of the Athabasca River and off Highway 93, Wapiti Campground which is located in Jasper National Park is a year-round campground offering private sites for tenting and electrical hook-up spots for RV's. In the summer months, the campground boasts 362 sites and during the winter months, it offers 93 sites. Visitors can enjoy amenities such as hot showers, electricity, and fire rings. The campground can accommodate most motorhomes and trailers.

Backcountry Camping & Cabins

Tonquinn Valley Backcountry Lodge

In Jasper National Park, on the continental divide, lies the Tonquin Valley Backcountry Lodge. This wilderness haven is 23 km from the nearest road, at the base of the world-famous Rampart Mountains, and on the shores of pristine Amethyst Lakes. Accessible only by foot or on horseback, the property is unique among Jasper's backcountry lodges and offers wilderness pack trips that everyone can enjoy. With towering peaks and lush meadows, Alberta's Tonquin Valley provides an authentic Canadian Rockies vacation.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$185 CAD

Shovel Pass Lodge

Experience a stay at the historic Shovel Pass Lodge, the oldest backcountry lodge in Jasper National Park operated by Skyline Trail Rides since 1990. It offers accommodation and meals packages for hikers of the Skyline Trail (meals only for hikers staying at Curator campsite and hiker's freight service to Shovel Pass Lodge). Spectacular mountain scenery, exceptional wildlife watching opportunities, and delicious home-cooked meals await you on this all-inclusive adventure in the Canadian Rockies.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$310 CAD

Jacques Lake Cabins

A Jasper Park patrol cabin, the Jacques Lake Cabin is the newest backcountry hut operated by the ACC. This cozy log cabin is perfect for families and small groups and is an accessible ski or snowshoe trip close to the Jasper townsite. The 12 km trail is relatively flat and the approach involves very little elevation gain. Note: the hut is only open in the winter months.

Wates-Gibson Hut

Tucked away in a corner of the spectacular Tonquin Valley, this large and well-equipped log cabin is a fantastic destination both summer and winter. There are a number of excellent alpine rock and mixed routes in the area as well as some delightful scrambles and many superb hiking opportunities. Something for everyone. This third version of the Wates-Gibson Memorial hut was built in 1959 after two previous structures in different locations were found to be inadequate for various reasons.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$50 CAD

Mt. Colin Centennial Hut

Situated beneath the impressive Southwest Face of Mt. Colin in Jasper National Park, this hut is used primarily as a base for climbers attempting one of several routes which exist on Colin or the neighbouring CR6. Scrambling and hiking opportunities in this tiny valley are somewhat limited, but you’ll definitely enjoy the peace and solitude the area offers. Solid limestone, sunny exposure and moderate grades make this an excellent destination for competent rock climbers.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$50 CAD
