Banff Visitor Centre

The Visitor Centre in downtown Banff brings you face to face with information on accommodation, activities, attractions, dining, and events. Want to stay an extra day but are wondering what you should do next? This is the first place that accommodation providers in the area notify when they are sold out. Open seven days a week and in all four seasons, friendly Visitor Experience Specialists provide the latest information on current events, concerts, live music, local festivals, and recent snowfall.

224 Banff Avenue, Banff, Alberta, T1L 1B3
403-762-8421

Canmore Visitor Information Centre

Located on Highway 1 in Canmore, Alberta, this Visitor Information Centre (VIC) is a great place to stop and get Banff and Jasper national park information, as well as great information on all of Alberta. Located next to the Legacy Trail trailhead, there is parking available for you to ride the trail. Very helpful and knowledgeable VIC staff can help with quick questions and trip planning information. There is also a free WiFi lounge and area to charge your devices.

2801 Bow Valley Trail, Canmore, Alberta, T1W 3A2
403-678-5277

Glacier Discovery Centre

Directly across from the Athabasca Glacier, is the Columbia Icefield Glacier Discovery Centre, where you can book a tour on an Ice Explorer snow coach that will take you right up onto the glacier. Located at the juncture of Banff and Jasper National Parks, the centre offers interpretive exhibits, a restaurant and cafeteria, an extensive patio, a gift shop, as well as scenic walks and self-guided trails. On the top floor is a boutique hotel, offering front row seating for viewing the glacier, not to mention superb stargazing. It is also the departure point for tours to the nearby Glacier Skywalk.

105 km south of Jasper, 130km north of Lake Louise
1-866-506-0515

Explore Hinton

Welcome to Hinton! Hinton Alberta is Western Canada's hub for untapped adventure and once in a lifetime experiences. Supported by a bustling business community and vibrant arts and culture scene, they invite you to come explore everything they have to offer. #ExploreHinton Alberta's Gateway to the Rockies.

309 Gregg Ave., Hinton, Alberta, T7V 2A7
780-865-7000

Info Jasper

The Visitor Information Centre is lead by our team of dedicated Travel Counsellors, eager to get you adventuring in Jasper National Park. For suggestions on where to stay, what to eat and what to do, seek guidance inside this historic log and stone house, located in the heart of downtown. Let one of their travel counsellors help you plan your next Jasper adventure!

414 Patricia St., Jasper, Alberta, T0E 1E0
1-780-852-6236

Waterton Lakes National Park Visitor Centre

The Waterton Lakes National Park Visitor Information Centre is open from mid-May through mid-October and is located on the Entrance Parkway. Parks Canada staff are available to answer questions and provide information on area activities and attractions.

1.5 km northeast of Waterton Townsite, off Highway 5.
403-859-5133

Yoho National Park Visitor Centre

The Parks Canada Visitor Centre at the entrance to the Field townsite is the best place for up-to-the-minute advice for Yoho visitors. Trail closures, weather updates, avalanche info, hiking advice and more can help make your Park experience safe and memorable. Revenues from Yoho National Park Visitor Entry and Service Fees help maintain national park facilities and services, ensuring that Yoho will remain pristine for future generations.

Trans-Canada Hwy, Field, BC V0A 1G0
(250) 343-6783
