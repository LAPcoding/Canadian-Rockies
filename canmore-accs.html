Campgrounds

Spring Creek RV Park

Spring Creek RV offers summer camping from May long weekend until Thanksgiving in October. Choose from 15 amp to 50 amp service. With over 100 RV sites, Spring Creek RV is situated in the heart of Canmore. Wander 10 minutes down the boardwalk to Main Street, where you will discover art galleries, restaurants, shops and more. Explore Banff National Park just 23 kms to the west, or explore Kananaskis just 21kms to the east.

Lac Des Arcs Campground

Lac Des Arcs Campground is found in Bow Valley Provincial Park, 14 km east of Canmore on Hwy. 1. There are about 30 open sites suitable for RVs and tents. Located on the shores of Lac Des Arcs which is popular with windsurfers. There are many activities available in the surrounding area, including hiking and biking trails, fishing and paddling.

Spray Lakes West Campground

Spray Lakes West Campground is found in Spray Valley Provincial Park, 16 km south of Canmore on the Smith Dorrien/Spray Lakes Trail (Hwy. 742). Once you visit this peaceful campground on the shores of the Spray Lakes Reservoir, you may never want to leave! There are sites suitable for both RVs and tents, many of which are close to the lake. Here you can enjoy hiking trails, boating, fishing, and wildlife watching in beautiful Rocky Mountain surroundings.

Willow Rock Campground

You’ll find Willow Rock Campground on the north side of the Trans Canada Highway, about 30 km from Canmore in Bow Valley Provincial Park. This large campsite contains both serviced and unserviced campsites in open and treed areas. The campground boasts plenty of amenities, including a playground, picnic tables, camp kitchen, and firepits. Other creature comforts include water, showers, and flush toilets on site. There are a few easy hikes close by and great fishing (rainbow trout, brown trout, and whitefish) on the Bow River. Please visit the website for the most up-to-date information on facility closures, service changes, programming updates and safety measures.

Backcountry Camping & Cabins

Big Elbow Backcounty Campground

This equestrian back country campground is found in Big Elbow Provincial Recreation Area. The trail head to access this site is in Little Elbow Provincial Recreation Area, 32 km southwest of Bragg Creek on Hwy. 66 (closed Dec. - May). It is located approximately 8.3 km from the Little Elbow Campground on the east half of the Elbow Loop. There are fire pits (firewood provided), hitching rails, pit/vault toilets, and secure food storage containers. This trail is 8.3km one way with 811 meters of elevation gain and is rated moderate.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$12 CAD

Jewell Bay Backcountry Campground

This scenic backcountry equestrian campground is found in Bow Valley Provincial Park on the northwest shore of Barrier Lake. The closest access is at the Barrier Dam Day Use Area, 30 km east of Canmore on Hwy. 1 and 12 km south on Hwy. 40. It is 3.9 km km from the trailhead and is rated easy. It can also be reached by boat. Here there are tent pads, hitching rails, corrals, pit/vault toilets, secure food storage lockers and firewood provided. Secondary access via Quaite Creek Trail/ Jewell Pass Trail from Heart Creek Day Use Area is 9.4 km and 340 m of elevation.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$12 CAD

Lillian Lake Backcountry Campground

Lillian Lake Backcountry Campground is in Spray Valley Provincial Park. The closest access is at Galatea Day Use Area, 42 km south of Hwy. 1 on Hwy. 40. It is 6.3 km from the trailhead via Galatea Creek Trail. Camp by this beautiful little mountain lake to enjoy the scenery or hike up to the Galatea Lakes. There are group fire pits, pit/vault toilets, secure food storage lockers, and raised tent platforms here. Secondary access from Ribbon Creek Trail/ Guinn's Pass Trail/ Galatea Creek Trail from Ribbon Creek Day Use Area is 14.9 km and 920 m of elevation gain. This trail is 6.3 km one way with 475 m meters of elevation gain and is rated moderate.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$12 CAD

Quaite Valley Backcountry Campground

This backcountry campground is found in Bow Valley Wildland Provincial Park. It can be accessed via the Quaite Valley Trail, which starts at the Heart Creek Day Use Area about 18 km east of Canmore on Hwy. 1. The campground is about 4.5 km from the trailhead. There are 20 tenting sites, with group firepits (firewood provided), pit/vault toilets, and secure food storage lockers available. Secondary access from Barrier Dam Day Use Area and is 8.6 km and 240 m of elevation. This trail is 4.7 km one way with 100 meters of elevation gain and is rated easy.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$12 CAD

Ribbon Falls Backcountry Campground

Ribbon Falls Backcountry Campground is in Spray Valley Provincial Park. The closest access is at Ribbon Creek Day Use Area in Evan-Thomas Provincial Recreation Area, 24 km south of Hwy. 1 off Hwy. 40. Enjoy the beauty of your surroundings on your way to this scenic spot. There are tent sites, pit/vault toilets and secure food storage facilities here. This trail is 9.1 km one way with 375 meters of elevation gain and is rated moderate-hard.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$12 CAD
