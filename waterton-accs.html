Hotels

Bear Mountain Motel

The Bear Mountain Motel looks forward to welcoming you and helping to make your visit all that it can be. They offer a simple, clean, affordable motel accommodation within the Park. They have single rooms to 3 room kitchenettes and oversized wooden patio furniture outside most rooms. The motel’s patio area includes an ice machine, microwave, two grills, and picnic tables that are available for all guests to use. The Bear Mountain Motel welcomes guests old and new.

After a day of adventure tramping on the trails, you will find fresh line-dried 100% cotton linens on your bed. To ensure a restful sleep they press the linens with lavender, and through your open window, the sound of Cameron Falls will help lull you to sleep.

Waterton Glacier Suites

At this family-owned and operated Lodge, guests of the Waterton Glacier Suites enjoy a mountain lodge feel and comfort in every room. Each room/suite has its own private balcony, fireplace, and Jacuzzi tub. Tastefully decorated with locally handcrafted furniture and Canadian artwork, their suites are great for family vacations, romantic getaways or simply to relax and enjoy all that Waterton has to offer all year round. Each of the 26 suites has been completely renovated, including flooring, wall coverings, furniture, lighting, fireplaces, bathrooms, art, 55″ TVs, and balconies. All walkways have been redone, and hardline internet and CAT 5 ethernet cable run through the entire building, providing fibre internet and internet TV.

This stately hotel offers 86 guest rooms and a traditional British atmosphere complete with Afternoon Tea, the Royal Stewart Dining Room, Windsor Lounge, and the Princess Gift Shop.

With the feeling of a country bed and breakfast and the comfort of a lodge, Crandell is a hotel experience to remember. Cabins & Lodges

Dungarvan Creek Vacation Rentals

Dungarvan Creek Vacation Rentals offers a modernized cottage and cabin for long or short-term rent within 10 mins of Waterton Lakes Park. Situated on a 40-acre rural property with panoramic views of the Rocky Mountains they offer a peaceful escape from the bustle of everyday life and are suitable for couples, families, and are LGBTQ friendly. Experience "nature on your doorstep" as there is a large pond that is home to a variety of wildfowl and attracts wildlife such as moose, deer, elk, and bear, and there are wildflowers in the fields in the summer months. At night the sky is alive with stars and on a clear night, you can view The Milky Way as this is a renowned "dark sky" area. The Cottage comfortably sleeps four people having two bedrooms, a living room, a fully equipped galley kitchen, and a shower room. It has gas central heating and is open year-round. The Cabin also sleeps four people with a separate shower room and 2nd bedroom. It has electric heating and is open from May to October. Small pets are allowed in the Cabin. Outside both the Cottage and Cabin there is plenty of space for parking and for children to play and each has a picnic table so you are welcome to bring your own small table BBQ. Please note that Dungarvan Creek is a non-smoking property.

$185 CAD

Crooked Creek Ranch

Crooked Creek Ranch is where your adventure begins, providing a quality bed and breakfast experience to all visitors. Crooked Creek Ranch sits on 40 acres in the SW corner of Alberta 10 minutes from the Montana, USA border crossing. Your adventure can begin with horseback riding, kayaking, canoeing, and hiking in Glacier National Park, Waterton Lakes National Park, or just walking the trails on the property. Amazing sunsets and wildlife viewing include grizzly bears, cougars, coyotes, deer, fox, hawks, and bald eagles. Campgrounds

Waterton Townsite

Located at the south end of the Waterton townsite, this campground offers 90 water, sewer and electrical sites, 45 electricity sites, as well as accessible, unserviced and walk-in tent sites. The campground also has hot showers, toilets, food storage and kitchen shelters; there are no individual fire pits. This open, mowed-lawn campground, exposed to winds from Upper Waterton Lake, is an excellent place for RVs or for those who wish to be within walking distance of the town's amenities. Reservations for the Townsite Campground are available, loops B and H only.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$24 CAD

Belly River

This self-registration campground offers 24 unserviced sites flush and dry privies, food storage, kitchen shelters, fire rings, and firewood (fire permit required). Bring your own drinking and cooking water, as no potable water is available at this campground. A pleasant area of mixed aspen forest, this primitive campground lies right beside the Belly River and is favoured by those campers wanting a more secluded experience.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$17 CAD

Crooked Creek Campground

Located 5.6 kilometres east of the Waterton National Park gates is Crooked Creek Campground, which offers 79 serviced (30-amp/pull-through/back-in) and unserviced campsites to the traveling public. Reservations are recommended. Amenities: free WiFi, shower facility & flush toilets, cook-shack (power, lighting, wood stove), on-site laundry, child-friendly, on-site cash-only store, firewood, ice, food & snack. Hiking, fishing, and golfing located nearby. Pet-Friendly, Dogs must be on a leash at all times. Open May 1st to September 30th. Visit the website for more details.

$30 CAD

Backcountry Camping & Cabins

Alderson Lake

This lake is usually accessed by hiking the Carthew-Alderson trail, but can be accessed directly by starting at Cameron Falls in the townsite. A long, steady, continuous climb through a diverse forest brings you to the aquamarine waters of Alderson Lake. The lake is set beneath the impressive cliffs of Mount Alderson, and a backcountry campsite is located here for those who want to spend the night. Permits can be purchased at the Parks Canada Visitor Centre. Fishing is also a popular activity at this pretty alpine lake. A Waterton Park license is required.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$11 CAD
Bertha Bay Campground

Enjoy this 5.0-km out-and-back trail near Waterton Park, Alberta. Generally considered a moderately challenging route, it takes an average of 1 h 38 min to complete. This is a very popular area for backpacking, birding, and camping, so you'll likely encounter other people while exploring. The trail is open year-round and is beautiful to visit anytime. Dogs are welcome, but must be on a leash.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$11 CAD

Boundary Bay Campground

If your family has a little bit of backcountry experience and your kids are keen for an overnight, the Boundary Bay Campground in Waterton is a great option. You’ll take the boundary trail and pass by Bertha Bay Campground on your way. This trail follows the Great Divide Trail to its southern terminus. You might see some thru hikers heading down to Boundary Bay to tag the terminus before beginning their northbound hike. The camp is extra special as it rests on the international boundary with the United States and Glacier National Park.

$11 CAD

Crandell Lake Campground

A fairly moderate, family-friendly hike from one of the major campgrounds in Waterton Lakes National Park to a beautiful lakeside backcountry campsite framed by stunning peaks in the Canadian Rockies. This is a beautiful route that is understandably popular with summer swimmers. Picturesque Crandell Lake and its lakeside campground can be reached by two routes in Waterton Lakes National Park — via a gentle 246-foot climb from the Akamina Parkway or this only-slightly-less-gentle 430-foot ascent from the Red Rock Parkway.

Goat Lake Campground

The Goat Lake trail is accessed from Red Rock Canyon. Cross the footbridge and begin your hike following the Snowshoe Trail for the first 4 km. During this section you will cross various creeks moving along generally flat terrain. Once you have arrived at the trail junction prepare to gain some major elevation fast, with a heart pounding 1,750 feet over just 3 km. Initially you will follow switchbacks up the mountainside, before the trail opens up giving you a view of where you are headed and where you have come. Persevere during this uphill slog and you will be rewarded once you have reached the basin. Beautiful Goat Lake is located beneath towering cliffs surrounded by spacious sub-alpine forests. Scan for Mountain Goats on the unforgiving cliffs above or catch some Cutthroat Trout dwelling in the emerald green waters below.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$11 CAD
