Deer Lodge

Rustic mountain lodging awaits you at this historic lodge in Banff National Park. With much of its original hand-hewn log structure and decor still in place, Deer Lodge is the classic definition of rustic mountain accommodations. Located just minutes by foot from the enchanting Lake Louise, Deer Lodge is a testament to a bygone era and retains much of its original hand-hewn log structure and decor. Built in 1923 as a teahouse, the lodge officially opened as a hotel in 1925. Deer Lodge was completely renovated, restored, and winterized in 1985. It is beloved by international and local guests for its unique rustic mountain accommodation.

Campgrounds

Waterfowl Lakes Campground

One of the largest campgrounds on the Icefields Parkway, beautiful Waterfowl Lakes offers plenty of forested, creekside and lakeside sites in the heart of the Canadian Rockies. Sites are available for tents or small RVs up to 8 meters (27 feet). Spectacular scenery and trails and the iconic Columbia Icefield are all nearby. More than 115 unserviced sites all have fire pits, some are wheelchair accessible and some are walk-in only. Waterfowl Campground offers dry and flush toilets, a Sani dump, shelters, water, food storage and convenient recycling bins. Interpretive programs are offered during the summer.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$23 CAD

Mosquito Creek

Located next to the Icefields Parkway in the heart of the Canadian Rockies, this rustic, forested campground is a good option for campers wanting an authentic camping experience. Sites are available for tents or small RVs up to 8 meters. Spectacular scenery, hiking trails and opportunities to view wildlife are close by. All 30 sites offer fire rings and access to water, sinks, picnic shelters and food storage. Mosquito Creek operates on a first-come, first-serve basis.

AVG RATE/NIGHT
$19 CAD

Lake Louise Tent Camping

Located just minutes from the Village of Lake Louise, this campground is perfect for anyone with soft-sided camping units. The forested sites here are close to local attractions, offer mountain views, and access to hiking and biking trails. Enjoy a riverside stroll right from your site or attend an evening interpretive program during the summer season. Most of the 200 unserviced sites have fire rings and all benefit from hot showers, flush toilets, picnic shelters, water, food storage and recycling bins. Cell coverage is available on some networks.

Lake Louise Trailer Campground

Located just minutes from the Village of Lake Louise, this campground is perfect for anyone needing space for larger units (12 metres or 40 feet) and/or people wanting power without full hook-ups. Forested sites next to the Bow River offer mountain views, year-round camping, and access to beautiful hiking, biking, and Nordic ski trails. Evening interpretive programs are offered during the summer season. All 180 sites offer electricity. There are also fire and no-fire rings, hot showers, flush toilets, water, a sani-dump, and convenient recycling bins. Cell coverage is available on some networks.

Taylor Lake Campground

The Taylor Lake hike in Banff National Park is an enjoyable hike up the lower slopes of Mount Bell to a beautiful mountain lake. The forest along the Taylor Lake trail is quite lush, in large part due to the creek the trail follows for most of its length. While we think Taylor Lake is a highly enjoyable hike near Lake Louise all year around, it is especially nice in the fall when the lake is surrounded by golden larch trees. On a clear day, the sight of a glowing turquoise Banff lake surrounded by craggy mountain peaks and golden larch trees is a breathtaking sight.
