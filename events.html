Banff Events

SnowDays Festival

Banff and Lake Louise comes alive with the annual Snowdays celebration. The streets of downtown Banff transform into larger-than-life winter galleries with giant snow sculptures to be found throughout; Lake Louise turns into a frozen wonderland with the sparkling ice sculptures of Ice Magic; the thrill of Skijoring and the Snowdays Play Zone come alive in the heart of Banff; and speciality cocktails and menus can be found at an array of restaurants for the Tribute Craft Spirits Festival.

January 19-30

Mountain Maddness Relay Race

This legendary relay race will take you and your team of five on the adventure of a lifetime! The race starts with a downhill ski from the top of Mt. Norquay. The race then picks up in the Recreation Grounds with running, ice skating, classic cross-country skiing and snowshoeing segments. Friends and fans will be there to cheer on participants of all ages and skill levels. But it’s not just about racing, teams will also be competing for the wackiest team name and best costumes.

February 3

Homegrown Art Show

Each spring Banff Town Hall hosts the work of artists from throughout the Bow Valley. This community exhibition is aimed at helping young, new and emerging artists share and celebrate their work with friends, families and other community members in a low-key setting. The exhibition kicks off with an open house event at Banff Town Hall on Friday, May 6 from 6 - 9 p.m.

May 6

National Indigenous Peoples Day

This is a day for all Canadians to recognize and celebrate the unique heritage, diverse cultures and outstanding contributions of First Nations, Inuit and Métis peoples. For generations, many Indigenous peoples and communities have celebrated their culture and heritage on or near this day due to the significance of the summer solstice as the longest day of the year.

June 21

Taste of Cultures

Experience a Taste of Cultures with locals from multicultural communities. Multicultural caterers from Banff that include Japanese, Filipino, Tibetan and Indian foods served in bite size format. Enjoy various performances while savoring traditional dishes and book a time with the human libraries. The human libraries is an opportunity to meet across cultures and hear stories related to the importance of Multicultural Day and their sense of place and belonging in Banff. Book a reservation to have a 20-minute conversation through the Banff Public Library.

June 24-27

Canada Day

The Town of Banff is the perfect place to celebrate Canada’s birthday on July 1. Fantastic family-friendly events run throughout the day, including our famous parade down Banff Avenue. From bands to balloon animals to birthday cake, we’ve got it all. The full-day celebrations are capped off with a spectacular evening fireworks display.

July 1

Melissa's Road Race

The Tradition Continues - Melissa’s Road Race is one of Canada’s most historic and popular running events and takes place on September 24th, 2022. Melissa’s features a 5k, 10k and Half Marathon on sensationally scenic routes in and around Banff National Park and routinely sells out. Since 1979, Melissa’s has been described as a colourful display of sport and fun in Banff National Park - famous for its entertainment along the course, contagious spirit, post-run party and long-sleeved commemorative running shirt featuring local art. Melissa’s has received multiple awards as Alberta’s Best Road Race and is a longstanding tradition in Banff.

September 24

Banff Pride

Take part in 11 days of drag shows, live music and a healthy dose of outdoor fun! Join us as locals and visitors alike celebrate diversity in Canada’s most iconic mountain town this fall.

September 30-October 10

Echoes of the Forest Halloween

You and your family will enjoy hot apple cider while listening to spooky stories and fireside songs by local musicians. You will have the opportunity to take part in some frightful games, create some spooky arts and crafts and connect with your community. Banff Public Library Storytime's being held at 1:30 p.m., 2:15 p.m., and 3 p.m. For all ages

October 30

Winter Solstice Family Celebration

Spend this Winter Solstice celebrating with your family at the Rundle Cabin. Do some crafts and enjoy some storytelling and tree decorating. The evening will wrap up with you and your family around the fire with a mug of hot chocolate or spiced tea and a delicious treat as we reflect and set intentions for the new season. For all ages

December 21

Canmore-Kananaskis Events

Canmore Winter Carnival

Canmore winters are fun and full of magic. Every February we come together as a community during the Canmore Winter Carnival to celebrate the best parts of our unique winter lifestyle. Join us for a weekend of indoor and outdoor events and experiences: ice carving competitions, kids’ ice carving , beaver tails eating competitions, the always entertaining pooch parade, log sawing and the amazing Snowy Owl Kid N’ Mutt races.

February 28-Mar 1

Canada Day

Community spirit shines through like in no other place in the Rockies during Canmore’s Canada Day Celebrations. On July 1, our community celebrates in a big way while retaining the small-town feel of togetherness.

July 1

Canmore Folk Music Festival

The Canmore Folk Music Festival is the longest running folk music festival in Alberta and one of its finest. Adhering to a philosophy and legacy of folk, world, blues and roots music, the festival since 1978 has attracted up to 21,000 music fans annually from across southern Alberta, western Canada, the northwestern USA and indeed the world.

August 5-7

Canmore Highland Games

Join us for a full slate of events comprising the Highland Games – pancake breakfast, highland dance demonstrations, a piping and drumming exhibition, drum majors workshop, heavy sports, tug of war, sheepdogs, clan tents, Celtic vendors, Scotch tasting, traditional food and beverages, food trucks, beer garden, massed bands and more.

September 3-4

Canmore Uncorked

Canmore Uncorked returns to celebrate the remarkable dining experiences in Canmore. The Festival consists of daily set-price menus and other culinary experiences at over 40 locations, as well as a series of signature events. Join fellow foodies for two magnificent weeks of food and drink.

September 29-October 9

Grizzly Canmore Ultra Marathon & Relay

The annual Grizzly Canmore Ultra Marathon and Relay is one of Canada's largest and most popular ultra races. Trail runners come from near and far to tackle the 50km course solo or team up for the two- or five-person relays. This destination trail race offers challenging trails surrounded by beautiful mountain landscapes.

October 8-9

New Year's Eve Party on the Pond

Join us and celebrate New Year’s Eve Canmore style… outdoors! Skate to music under a starry sky drinking hot chocolate and warming up by the bonfires with hundreds of other families. The gorgeous mountains make an incredible backdrop for an unforgettable pyrotechnics show.

December 31

Hinton Events

Winter Magic Festival

The Town of Hinton helps coordinate a magical mixture of art, food, recreation, and family fun activities organized by community groups. This winter celebration is a seasonal highlight and has been running for 35 years.

February 5-27

Fohn Festival & Canada Day Celebrations

Hinton's signature event! The Föhn Festival is Hinton's opportunity to celebrate the region's historical significance, connect with neighbours, understand the cultures that make up our community, increase a sense of community pride and showcase our rich diversity to new residents and visitors. The Föhn Festival Society of Hinton is a non-profit organization made up entirely of volunteers. Canada Day (July 1st) celebrations include parade, pancake breakfast, Canada Day cake, and fireworks (June 30th), as well as great entertainment in Green Square the following day.

July 1

Wild Mountain Music Festival

Weekend passes are available with rv/tent camping on-site! The festival features 2 performance stages, beer gardens, food and art vendors, and kids activities. Festival Shuttle Bus to and from Hinton (several pickup/drop-off spots), WMMF General Store on-site for all your festival needs. Weekend passes are available.

July 15-17

Jasper Events

Jasper in January

Get ready to celebrate the best that winter has to offer. For three decades, Jasper in January has been the nexus of joyful mountain culture and having good times in any weather. This year, we invite you to rediscover this playful festival with the help of your favourite seasonal activities, now featuring some fresh new twists.

January 13-29

Jasper Beer & Spirits Festival

Alberta Beer Festival’s Mission is to provide the ultimate beer & spirit experience! Each of our 6 events provides a forum for breweries and distilleries to share their stories, passions, and products with the beer enthusiasts of Western Canada. ABF strives to have a positive impact in the beer/spirit industry, with local charities, and within our community.

February 10-12

Devour! The Canadian Rockies Food Film Festival

Discover a love for food and film (and wine) this winter in the grandest of natural settings, Jasper National Park. Join us in appreciating talented filmmakers and high profile chefs, from all around the globe. Cozy up in the Rockies while you celebrate cinema, indulge in exquisite food & wine, and experience all Fairmont Jasper Park Lodge has to offer.

March 31-April 2

Jasper Pride Festival

Join us in Jasper National Park for the only gay ski week in the Canadian Rockies. We’re talking ten days of epic Pride events, action-packed activities and unlimited skiing—all in the spirit of coming together, proud and free.

April 1-10

Banff Jasper Relay

The Banff Jasper Relay is a 15 person relay race completing the 260km distance between Banff and Jasper. It is the most beautiful relay in the world, and they are on a mission to end brain tumours by raising awareness and money for the Brain Tumour Foundation of Canada. Following the race is the most epic party in Jasper to celebrate your accomplishments and included with your registration - dinner, awards ceremony, music, and fun.

June 4

Canada Day

Join us at Commemoration Park to experience a day full of Jasper traditions. Begin your morning with a Pancake Breakfast sponsored by the Municipality of Jasper followed by the official Flag Raising and singing of the national anthem at the Jasper Royal Canadian Legion. The return of the beloved Canada Day Parade will see our community come together to showcase everything that makes Jasper the place to be.

July 1

Summer Sounds

Soak up the start of summer in Jasper with a month of live music from JUNO Award nominee, Neil Harnett.

June 2-September 13

Jasper Dark Sky Festival

As daylight hours begin to recede, October is the ideal time to celebrate the skies with the annual Jasper Dark Sky Festival, an ever-growing festival aimed at connecting all ages to our universe and beyond.

October 14-23

Christmas in November

Experience a food and wine celebration set in the land of a million Christmas trees. From the moment you drive through the “Home for the Holidays’ gates of Fairmont Jasper Park Lodge until the minute you leave, you’ll be swept up in the magic of Christmas. For over 30 years, Christmas in November has been the most anticipated event of the year at Fairmont Jasper Park Lodge. With a variety of presenters, activities and sessions to attend, this event has surely been one to remember.

November 4-13

Lake Louise Events

Ice Magic Festival

The Ice Magic Ice Carving Competition is a perfectly magical winter experience that awaits you in Lake Louise where ice serves as a fascinating medium of artistry and architectural design. Watch incredible ice carvings take shape under the precise hands of an all-Canadian cast of skilled artists.

January 19-30

Canada Day

Join them this Canada Day at Canada's Natural Playground! Their Banded Peak Base Camp will be open (11:00am - 6:30pm), which will showcase some Canadian culinary treats! The Base Camp has a spacious patio in their sunny base area, and there's no lift ticket required to visit. Whitehorn Bistro will also be open (11:00am - 4:30pm), which will have a special Wine & Cheese offering. Enjoy a Canadian Cheese plate for $25 and half price select British Columbia VQA bottled wines. A lift ticket or pass is required to visit.

July 1

Lake Louise Alpine World Cup

The Lake Louise Alpine World Cup is notorious for its epic early season snow, the proximity fans have with the athletes and the intimacy of the hamlet of Lake Louise, nestled pristinely in Banff National Park. Fans from across the globe will watch as these athletes take every risk to achieve the ultimate goal. Standing on the podium means dropping 800 vertical metres downhill at speeds up to 130 km/h on a three kilometre track in just about a minute and a half. Just as gritty is watching these athletes navigate the gates of a super-G as they do everything they can to keep their skis against the snow and carve through the ice. Fast and exciting, yes.

November 25-December 4

Waterton Events

Winterfest

Experience the wonders of winter in Waterton Lakes National Park ! Participate in interpretive snowshoe hikes to Crandell lake, horse drawn carriage rides, documentary viewings, avalanche awareness, and more indoor and outdoor fun for your entire family.

February 17-20

Waterton Wildflower Festival

We invite you to step into a wildflower wonderland, a veritable bouquet of wildflowers in the wildflower capital of Canada. The spectacular setting of Waterton Lakes National Park is home to more than 1,000 vascular plant species including half of all wildflower species found in Alberta. Of those, 175 species are listed as rare in Alberta and 20 are only found in Waterton.

June 13-18

Canada Day

Connect with history and nature this Canada Day in Waterton Lakes National Park. Enjoy free admission on Friday July 1, and check out Parks Canada’s special activities with your friends and family.

July 1

Waterton Wildlife Weekend

Because of these geological and ecological factors, Waterton holds immense wildlife diversity. For its size of just over 505 sq. kilometers, Waterton Lakes National Park holds the most wildlife diversity than any other national park in Canada. In fact, it is very rare to not see some sort of wildlife in Waterton, whether it be a mule deer, elk, big horn sheep or black bear. Out of multitude of questions and general curiosity about the diversity of wildlife in Waterton, the Waterton Wildlife Weekend was born.
